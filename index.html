With each passing year and each subsequent legislative session, it gets harder and harder to get an abortion in Texas.

Roe v. Wade legalized abortion in 1973. Abortion should be safe, legal, accessible in a woman’s community, and available without shame or pressure; but lawmakers have passed dozens of laws to move us backwards and widen the gap between a patient and their ability to get an abortion.

Policy

  1. 01/01/1973

    All women have access to abortion, and Medicaid covers abortion.

  2. Hyde Amendment

    01/01/1976

    The Hyde Amendment stops federal funding from paying for abortion, meaning you can't use Medicaid or other federal health insurance to pay for abortion

  3. Abortion refusals

    01/01/1977

    Hospitals and individuals can refuse to allow or provide abortions regardless of the reason.

  4. Physician-only requirement

    01/01/1985

    Only a physician licensed by the state may provide abortion care.

  5. No Medicaid funding

    01/01/1989

    Medicaid cannot cover an abortion except in cases of life endangerment.

  6. Parental notification

    01/01/1999

    A woman under age 18 cannot get an abortion until 48 hours after the physician tells at least one of her parents either in person or by phone.

  7. Mandatory delay and medically inaccurate info

    01/01/2003

    A woman must wait 24 hours before the procedure is provided. The doctor must also give the woman governmentwritten materials about abortion, including realistic photos of what a fetus looks like at two-week intervals, and must talk about medical assistance benefits for pre-natal care and childbirth. Doctors are required to give patients medically inaccurate information, such as telling them that abortions can cause breast cancer.

  8. Anti-abortion resources

    01/01/2003

    Doctors must give women a state-mandated list of groups for counseling that includes anti-abortion organizations. These organizations give women medically inaccurate information and try to pressure them out of having an abortion.

  9. Clinic requirement

    01/01/2003

    Requires abortions performed at or after 16 weeks to be performed in a licensed hospital or ambulatory surgical center.

  10. No contracts with abortion facilities

    01/01/2005

    Prohibits the Department of Human Services from contracting with health care facilities or individuals who provide abortion care or are affiliated with facilities that provide abortion care.

  11. State funding for CPCs

    01/01/2005

    State funding for crisis pregnancy centers (CPCs), a type of non-profit organization established to counsel pregnant women against having an abortion.

  12. Additional requirements for minors seaking abortion

    01/01/2006

    Requires a parent’s written consent to be notarized before a woman under the age of 18 can get an abortion.

  13. Silencing abortion care at the Department of Human Services

    01/01/2011

    Prohibits the Department of Human Services and its employees from referring women to organizations that provide or “promote” abortion care.

  14. State funding for anti-choice organizations

    01/01/2011

    State funding for a “Choose Life” license-plate program that sends money to an anti-abortion organization.

  15. Required display of sonogram

    01/01/2011

    Requires a doctor to perform a sonogram on a woman seeking an abortion at least 24 hours prior to performing the abortion. The doctor must display the sonogram image in front of the woman, provide a verbal explanation of the image (including descriptions of the fetus, its heart activity, and its internal organs), and provide an opportunity for the woman to hear the fetal heartbeat.

  16. Increased building standards for clinics

    01/01/2013

    Requires health centers that provide abortions to meet building standards designated for ambulatory surgical centers.

  17. Admitting privledges for abortion providers

    01/01/2013

    Requires doctors who provide abortions to become part of the admitting staff at a nearby hospital that provides obstetrics/ gynecological care.

  18. Hospitals are free to refuse admitting privileges

    01/01/2013

    Nothing in the regulations requires hospitals to grant this privilege

  19. Bans abortion after 20 weeks

    01/01/2013

    Bans abortion after 20 weeks, unless the woman’s life or health is at risk or in the case of a severe fetal anomaly

  20. Restrictions on medication abortion

    01/01/2013

    Prohibits anyone other than a physician from dispensing abortion-inducing medication. Also requires that the woman and physician both be present at a licensed abortion facility when the abortion pill is taken. And the physician must schedule a followup visit no more than fourteen days after the abortion-inducing medication is taken.

  21. Private Insurance Ban

    01/01/2017

    Bans private insurance coverage of abortion

  22. Restrictions on method

    01/01/2017

    Bans a specific standard method of abortion

  23. Reporting requirements

    01/01/2017

    Imposes requirements that providers who perform abortion report information about the abortions to the state
